About me

I am a 3rd-year PhD student in statistics at CREST (Center for Research in Economics and Statistics), Paris, under the supervision of Pierre Alquier, currently research scientist at RIKEN AIP in Tokyo. My main research interests lie in variational inference for approximating intractable posterior distributions in Bayesian statistics and machine learning. More precisely, my work focuses on the statistical properties of variational approximations and the design of efficient algorithms for computing them in an online fashion. I'm also interested in robust statistics, and I investigate Maximum Mean Discrepancy based estimators as learning rules that are robust to model misspecification. I provide teaching sessions in mathematics at ENSAE Paris. Prior that, I graduated with the MVA Master program in machine learning and computer vision from Ecole Normale Supérieure Paris-Saclay and obtained the engineering degree of ENSAE Paris.
